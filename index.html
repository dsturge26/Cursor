// 01 the mandate, as I read it

Codify what works, without slowing the ship.

As demand accelerates, the job is to turn Cursor's momentum into repeatable, high-impact deployment outcomes for strategic enterprise customers. That means leading a team of AI Deployment Managers, owning the post-sale experience end to end, and partnering with Sales and Field Engineering so customers realize measurable engineering and business value, all while preserving the low-friction, developer-first ethos that got Cursor here.

// 02 the first 90 days, then scale

Learn the motion. Codify it. Scale the team.

High-agency and hands-on early, building the scalable operating model the whole time. Select a phase:

days 1-30

Listen and ride along.

Understand the team and the motion before changing either. Establish what "good deployment" looks like and what's slowing it down.

Meet the team

1:1s with every AI Deployment Manager, plus Sales and Field Engineering, to understand the current motion, strengths, and friction.

Ride live deployments

Shadow active enterprise rollouts to see firsthand where time-to-value is won or lost, and what separates a great deployment from a stalled one.

Baseline the signals

Establish the metrics that matter: adoption and usage depth, account health, expansion signals, and deployment cycle time.

Find the patterns

Identify the rollout, prompt, and context-configuration practices already working, the raw material worth codifying.

days 31-60

Codify what works.

Turn the patterns into lightweight, repeatable playbooks that raise quality and consistency without adding friction.

Deployment playbooks

Codify rollout strategy, prompt design, context configuration, and internal enablement into lightweight, scalable guides that preserve speed.

Health and risk model

Define the account-health, usage, and risk signals that flag a deployment drifting, early enough for the team to act.

Escalation path

Stand up a clear escalation route for complex deployments and high-stakes enterprise accounts, with me as the backstop.

Expansion motion

Tie expansion-pipeline generation to demonstrated customer outcomes, so growth follows value, not pressure.

addresses:lightweight processprompt + context best practicesrisk mitigation
days 61-90

Scale the team and the loop.

Operationalize coaching and the product feedback loop so quality holds as the team and customer base grow.

Coaching cadence

Onboarding, skill development, and a performance rhythm that levels up every AI Deployment Manager as demand scales.

Field to Product loop

Aggregate what enterprise developers need into structured input for Product, Engineering, and Marketing to influence roadmap and adoption.

// 03 how I'd operate

Principles, not bureaucracy.

Codify, don't ceremonialize

I build with agentic tooling daily and shipped this page myself. I can whiteboard with developers, not just talk about them.

principle_03

High agency in ambiguity

I drove org-wide AI adoption with no direct authority. I default to action, then refine with evidence.

principle_04

Two languages, one room

Comfortable discussing context windows with an engineer and business value with a VP or CTO in the same conversation.
