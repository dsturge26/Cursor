            Approach

            How I Think About AI Deployment

            AI deployment is not just tool access. Adoption quality depends on workflow fit, developer trust, enablement, measurement, and executive alignment. The best deployments connect technical usage to business outcomes. Successful rollout requires tight feedback loops between users, field teams, product, and leadership.

            In practice, that means treating an AI coding-tool deployment as both a customer transformation motion and an operating system for learning. Sales, Field Engineering, Product, Engineering, Security, and customer leadership need a shared view of the use cases, blockers, adoption signals, and moments where workflow design either creates trust or introduces friction.

            Project rationale

            Why I Built This

            I built this as a lightweight project to demonstrate how I think about enterprise AI deployment and to get closer to the developer workflow through hands-on AI-assisted development. My background is in enterprise sales, renewals, operations, AI adoption, and business-side SDLC participation, and I am actively developing my own app using AI tools.

            The workflow behind this project is intentionally hands-on: VS Code, Claude, Codex, and vanilla app development patterns, used from the perspective of a deployment leader who wants to understand how these tools feel inside real developer work.

            The project reflects how I would approach a customer conversation: diagnose readiness, identify adoption risk, connect technical behavior to business outcomes, and turn the plan into a practical operating cadence.
